Andrew Tredennick

Bio

Andrew Tredennick confronts dynamic models with data to explore, predict, and understand. He is currently a Research Scientist at Metrum Research Group, using mechanistic models and state-of-the-art statistical approaches to accelerate the drug development process. Andrew has completed projects on early warning signals for disease re-emergence and forecasting models of emerging infectious diseases, like COVID-19. He works from home in beautiful Laramie, Wyoming.

Education

Colorado State University | Fort Collins, CO

Ph.D. in Ecology | 2014

Texas Tech University | Lubbock, TX

B.S. in Biology (Minor in Chemistry) | 2006

Experience

Western EcoSystems Technology | Team Lead & Consulting Statistician | February 2019 - September 2022

Center for the Ecology of Infectious Diseases, University of Georgia | Postdoctoral Research Associate | June 2018 - February 2019

Utah State University | Postdoctoral Researcher | June 2014 - June 2018

Awards
